* * Tokyo * *

The origin of the city of Tokyo stretches back some 400 years. Originally named Edo, the city started to flourish after Tokugawa Ieyasu established the Tokugawa Shogunate here in 1603. As the center of politics and culture in Japan, Edo grew into a huge city with a population of over a million by the mid-eighteenth century.

* * Hokkaido * *

Hokkaido's origins can be traced back to millions of years ago when it was formed through intense volcanic activity, shaping its rugged terrain and natural beauty. Over time, this land became home to the indigenous Ainu people, who developed their unique culture and way of life in harmony with the island's environment.
